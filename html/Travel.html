CHANG SHA

Changsha, also known as Star City and Chu-Han City, is also the capital of Hunan. Shanshuizhou City is the most precious gift given to Changsha by nature. This beautiful land has written too many beautiful chapters in history: Du Fu, the poet saint, left a beautiful scenery in the south of the Yangtze River, and won the eternal swan song in the flowering season; Zhang Shi and Zhu Xi taught twice in Yuelu Academy in Song Dynasty. Mao Zedong, a great man of another generation, stopped in orange island, and was full of enthusiasm and excited about words.

WU HAN

Wuhan (hereinafter referred to as "Han"), also known as "Jiangcheng", is the capital of Hubei Province, a vice provincial city, a mega city, and a central city in Central China.Wuhan is located in the east of Jianghan Plain and the middle reaches of the Yangtze River. The Yangtze River and its largest tributary Hanjiang River meet in the city, forming a pattern of three towns across the river in Wuhan.

JING DE ZHEN

Jingdezhen City, also known as "porcelain capital", is a prefecture level city in Jiangxi Province, located in the northeast of Jiangxi Province. Jingdezhen is the porcelain capital of the world and the cradle of China's helicopter industry. It is one of the first batch of 24 famous historical and cultural cities announced by the State Council and a national class a open area. During the period of the Republic of China, it was once known as the four famous towns in China together with Foshan in Guangdong, Hankou in Hubei and Zhuxian in Henan.
